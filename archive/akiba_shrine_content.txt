'''Akiba Shrine''' (秋葉神社) is a [[:en:Shinto shrine|Shinto shrine]] located in {{ill|Matsugaya, Taito, Tokyo|ja|東京都台東区松が谷|12=simple|13=User:Immanuelle/Matsugaya, Taito, Tokyo|ja_comment=jawiki link invalid}}.
== History ==
Originally named *Chinka-sha* (鎮火社), the shrine was established in [[:en:1870|1870 AD]] (Meiji 3) following a great fire in late [[:en:1869|1869 AD]] (Meiji 2). The shrine was founded by imperial order of [[:en:Emperor Meiji|Emperor Meiji]] at the present-day site of [[:en:Akihabara Station|Akihabara Station]] ({{ill|Kanda Hanaokacho, Chiyoda, Tokyo|ja|東京都千代田区神田花岡町|12=simple|13=User:Immanuelle/Kanda Hanaokacho, Chiyoda, Tokyo|ja_comment=jawiki link invalid}}). The shrine was dedicated to three deities: {{ill|Kagu-tsuchi|lt=Kagu-tsuchi|lt=Kagutsuchi|ja|火産霊大神|12=simple|13=User:Immanuelle/Kagutsuchi|ja_comment=jawiki link invalid}}, the god of fire; {{ill|Mizuhanome|ja|水波能売神|12=simple|13=User:Immanuelle/Mizuhanome|ja_comment=jawiki redirects to ミヅハノメ}}, the goddess of water; and {{ill|Haniyasu-hiko and Haniyasu-hime|lt=Haniyasu-hiko and Haniyasu-hime|lt=Haniyasu|ja|埴山毘売神|12=simple|13=User:Immanuelle/Haniyasu|ja_comment=jawiki link invalid}}, the goddess of earth.
During the [[:en:Edo period|Edo period]], frequent large fires in [[:en:Edo|Edo]] led to widespread worship of {{ill|Akiba Gongen|ja|秋葉権現|12=simple|13=User:Immanuelle/Akiba Gongen|qq=}}, a deity enshrined at {{ill|Mount Akiba (Shizuoka)|ja|秋葉山|12=simple|13=User:Immanuelle/Mount Akiba (Shizuoka)|1=Mount Akiba (Shizuoka)|comment=changed title based on wikidata|WD=Q11595833}} as a protector against fire. However, despite popular belief, the establishment of this shrine was not directly related to Akiba Gongen (although some historical records claim that the [[:en:Tokyo Prefecture (1868–1943)|Tokyo Prefecture]] enshrined Akiba Gongen, this is considered a misinterpretation based on the historical context). Nonetheless, many people mistakenly believed that the shrine was dedicated to Akiba Gongen and referred to it as "Akiba-sama" or "Akiba-san." The open space around the shrine, which served as a firebreak, became known as *Akiba no Hara* (秋葉の原) or *Akiba-ppara* (秋葉っ原). The pronunciation "Akiba" is a local dialect variation, whereas the original name "Akihabara" should be read as *Akiha*.
Over time, the shrine's name changed from *Chinka-sha* to *Akiba-sha*. In [[:en:1888|1888 AD]] (Meiji 21), when the [[:en:Nippon Railway|Nippon Railway]] extended its railway line (now part of the [[:en:Tōhoku Main Line|Tōhoku Main Line]]) from the present-day [[:en:Ueno Station|Ueno Station]] to Akihabara Station, the land of Akiba no Hara was sold off, and the shrine was relocated to its current site.{{sfn|Tokyo Shrine Association|qq=}} The shrine was later renamed *Akiba Shrine* in [[:en:1930|1930 AD]] (Shōwa 5).

Another Akiba Shrine is located at 4-21 {{ill|Taito, Taito, Tokyo|ja|台東区台東|12=simple|13=User:Immanuelle/Taito, Taito, Tokyo|ja_comment=jawiki redirects to 台東 (台東区)|1=Taitō|comment=changed title based on wikidata|WD=Q11411946}}. It stands on a section of the former estate of the [[:en:Satake clan|Satake clan]], the lords of {{ill|Akita Domain|ja|秋田藩|12=simple|13=User:Immanuelle/Akita Domain|ja_comment=jawiki redirects to 久保田藩|1=Kubota Domain|comment=changed title based on wikidata|WD=Q1067720}}, and is commonly known as *Satake Akiba Shrine*.
{{efn|According to information provided by the shopping district association, this Akiba Shrine was also relocated during the Meiji period from another location and has no direct historical connection to the Satake clan.{{sfn|Satake Shopping District Promotion Association|qq=}}|qq=}}
